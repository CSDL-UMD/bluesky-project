How do you decide what is reliable or not?

We do not decide what is reliable or not. Instead, we rely on a third-party assessment by NewsGuard. Their rating system is compiled by a team of professional journalists, who assess the credibility and transparency of a news website on a scale from 0 to 100. News websites that score at or above 60 adhere to basic standards of credibility and transparency and are considered reliable, while scores below 60 typically indicate that a news source does not basic journalistic standards and should thus be deemed unreliable.

How is the data collected?

We collect data from the Bluesky Firehose, which provides a stream of all messages posted by Bluesky users in real time. We only consider posts that include a link to a news website rated by the NewsGuard rating system. This includes all news sources accounting for 95% of online engagement.

How often is the chart updated?

A database entry is made every hour, and then the chart is updated immediately after.

How was the visualization developed?

The visualization is created using Shiny and lets_plot. The data is stored in a PostgreSQL database, and we use Pandas for data processing and transformation.

Is the code opensource?

Yes, you can find the code on GitHub.

Who created MurkySky?

MurkySky is a project of the Computational Sociodynamics Lab at the University of Maryland. Vikas Reddy developed the web app frontend and the data collection backend, under the coordination of Giovanni Luca Ciampaglia.

What influenced the creation of MurkySky?

The Iffy Quotient project has played a significant role in shaping our project, and their commitment to transparency and accuracy has greatly influenced our project's direction.
