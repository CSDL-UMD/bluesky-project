Skywatch FAQ

How is the data collected?

We collect data from the Bluesky firehose, which provides a stream of all messages posted by Bluesky users in real time. We only consider posts that include a link to a news website. Utilizing the NewsGuard rating system, each link is classified as reliable or unreliable.

How often is the chart updated?

A database entry is made every hour, and then the chart is updated immediately after.

What does the Emoji represent?

The emoji represents the average proportion of unreliable news links over the last 7 days. The average value is matched with the corresponding emoji using the following scale:

  • [0.00, 0.10]: ☀️
  • (0.10, 0.20]: 🌤️
  • (0.20, 0.30]: ⛅️
  • (0.30, 0.40]: ☁️
  • (0.40, 0.50]: 🌦️
  • (0.50, 0.60]: 🌧️
  • (0.60, 0.70]: 🌩️
  • (0.70, 0.80]: ⛈️
  • (0.80, 0.90]: 🌨️
  • (0.90, 1.00]: 🌪️

How do you decide what is reliable or not?

We rely on a third-party assessment by NewsGuard. Their rating system is compiled by a team of professional journalists, who assess the credibility and transparency of a news website on a scale from 0 to 100. News websites that score at or above 60 adhere to basic standards of credibility and transparency and are considered reliable, while scores below 60 typically indicate that a news source is deemed unreliable and in violation of basic journalistic standards.

How was the visualization developed?

The visualization is created using Shiny for Python code and the lets_plot library, the Python counterpart to the popular 'ggplot2' library in R. The initial steps involve fetching data from the PostgreSQL database, with subsequent processing and transformation into a Pandas DataFrame (df). This DataFrame serves as the foundation for the visualization, containing essential information such as timestamps, total links, and NewsGuard classifications. The lets_plot library is used for creating the charts, utilizing lp.geom_area functions to represent various areas within the plot, each corresponding to categories such as 'Reliable', 'Unreliable', and 'Total'.
